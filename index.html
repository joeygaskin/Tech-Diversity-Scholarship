The Tech Diversity Scholarship is the product of a public-private partnership of organizations committed to providing access to technology education for all. The scholarship is designed to remove financial barriers for people who are underrepresented in the technology industry, including but not limited to: women, people of color, military personnel and people with disabilities. Financially-eligible students will be considered and may be eligible to receive a full-ride scholarship to attend a full-time code school and take the first step toward a fulfilling career in technology.

The Commitment:

Education Partners

The schools participating in this effort are committing—with the support of both private companies and the government—to reserve spots in in every class for those underrepresented in tech, who might not be able to study at a code school otherwise. For The Iron Yard, that’s over 1300 students and $18 million in scholarships over three years.

The Commitment:

Employment Partners

Companies participate in a variety of ways, from contributing financially towards scholarships to supporting the in-class experience and, most importantly, building hiring and apprenticeship programs for scholarship recipients.

The Support Organizations

The White House has endorsed this program and encourages as many cities as possible to participate. In each city where these scholarships are offered we partner with local government (Mayors, economic development offices and workforce development offices) and state government (Governors, statewide economic and workforce offices) to identify possible participants, create awareness and partner with existing programs working to accomplish the same goals.

The Commitment:

Accountability

The outcomes of the scholarship program will be audited by a third party. This will enable the Tech Diversity Scholarship program to be transparent with all of the outcome data. The goal is to always be focused on the best interest of the students, not special interests.
